My Design + Code Journey

01

The Threshold

"What was the specific moment this quarter where 'code' clicked for you?"

In the first few weeks, it was really hard for me to understand what ID and class meant and code after the first two weeks when we started learning about CSS, I finally understood what class and CSS were meant for and why we use them in code it was a moment where I started fully understanding code overall as well, and where everything started to make sense to me.

02

The Pit

"What was the hardest assignment? How did you get yourself out of the 'pit of despair'?"

For me, the hardest assignment was probably the field guide mainly because I decided to make my field guide to super complicated and long it took me forever and I really was just so sick of it not only was did it take forever to code. It took forever to design as well. I was able to get myself out of the pit of despair by taking a lot lots of breaks and just giving myself time that's why I am very thankful for that. We are able to turn in assignments two weeks after they are due that really helped me.

03

The Pivot

"Look at your original 'Moodz and Vibes.' How does your final portfolio compare? Did you stick to the plan or pivot? Why?"

Honestly, I feel like I have an aesthetic in the way I design I feel like my work as a whole has a sort of vibe to it that you can instantly tell it is my work. I think my portfolio definitely fits that vibe and definitely shows me as a whole and all my work. I use very bright colors and all of my designs and very pastel cute see colors and design choices and I definitely think that my final portfolio ended up showing that I definitely stick to the plan because I wanted my final portfolio to look like it is my work and my portfolio is who I am as a whole if I was a design.

04

The Process

"What would you change about how you approached this course? (e.g., time management, reading documentation, asking for help)"

Honestly, I probably wouldn't have approached this course in a different way. If I had to take it again I think from the beginning, I had a very positive mindset, which helped me get through all of the assignments and all of the hard parts of coding. I feel like if I had to do this course again I would give myself more time if possible to do assignments and just fully grasp all the concepts and thoroughly learn everything.

05

The Future

"How do you see this hybrid skillset (Design + Code) impacting your future practice?"

In the future, I want to become a product, designer and I think that it would be very helpful to just know how to code my designs. Also, I feel like it'll definitely be a plus to be able to have on my résumé that I know how to code and if I do need to fix things in my designs like specific placements on a website, I can easily do that because I know code
