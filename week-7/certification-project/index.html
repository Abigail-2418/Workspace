Overview

Settlers of Catan is a multiplayer board game where players assume the roles of settlers establishing colonies on the island of Catan. The objective is to be the first to reach 10 victory points through building settlements, cities, and roads, while trading resources and developing your economy.

The game board consists of hexagonal tiles representing different terrain types that produce resources. Players collect these resources to construct buildings and expand their civilization across the island.

Success in Catan requires balancing resource management, strategic placement, clever trading, and adapting to the luck of the dice. No two games are ever the same due to the randomized board setup and dynamic player interactions.

Resources of Catan

Catan features five essential resources, each produced by specific terrain types. Understanding resource production and scarcity is fundamental to developing a winning strategy. Each resource has unique uses and varying availability on the board.

Resources are collected when the number on a terrain hex matches the dice roll. The number tokens show probability dots indicating how frequently that number is rolled (six and eight being most common, two and twelve being rarest).

Strategic settlement placement near high-probability numbers ensures steady resource income, while diversification protects against poor dice rolls and gives you more trading flexibility.

3

4

Watch the robber: Protect your most productive hexes and block opponents

Settlements & Cities

Settlements are the foundation of your civilization in Catan. Each settlement is worth 1 victory point and produces one resource card whenever the adjacent hex's number is rolled. Settlements must be placed at the intersection of three hexes with at least two edges of separation from other settlements.

Cities are upgraded settlements worth 2 victory points that produce double the resources. Upgrading to cities is essential for late-game resource production and reaching the 10-point victory threshold. Cities require significant investment but provide exponential returns.

Placement strategy is permanent and crucial. Your initial two settlements determine your resource income for the entire game. Choose locations that provide resource diversity, high probability numbers, and access to ports for favorable trading ratios.

Construction Costs

Settlement (1 VP):

  • 1 Lumber

  • 1 Brick

  • 1 Wheat

  • 1 Wool
City (2 VP - upgrades settlement):

  • 3 Ore

  • 2 Wheat

Settlement Placement Priorities

  • High probability numbers: Target intersections touching 6s and 8s
  • Resource diversity: Access to multiple resource types from one settlement
  • Port access: 2:1 specialty ports or 3:1 generic ports provide trading advantages
  • Expansion potential: Leave room for future settlements and road networks
  • Block opponents: Deny prime locations to competitors when possible

City Upgrade Timing

Upgrade to cities when you have steady ore and wheat production. Cities double your resource intake, making them crucial for mid-to-late game acceleration. Prioritize upgrading settlements on high-probability hexes first to maximize return on investment.

Roads & Expansion

Roads connect your settlements and cities, enabling expansion across the island. Each road must connect to your existing road network or settlement, creating continuous paths across hex edges. Roads are essential for claiming the Longest Road bonus and accessing new building locations.

The Longest Road achievement awards 2 victory points to the player with the longest continuous road of at least 5 segments. This achievement can change hands during the game and often becomes a crucial swing factor in close matches.

Strategic road placement involves planning multiple moves ahead. Block opponent expansion routes while securing your own path to valuable building sites. Roads are your cheapest construction option, making them ideal for early game territorial control.

Road Network Strategies

1

Plan your route: Identify valuable settlement locations and build roads toward them

2

3

4

Longest Road pursuit: Build continuous networks to claim or steal this 2-point bonus

Trading System

Trading is the lifeblood of Catan's economy and often determines game outcomes. Players can trade resources with each other through negotiation or trade with the bank at fixed ratios. Successful traders leverage abundance and scarcity to manipulate the resource market in their favor.

Player trading occurs on your turn and has no restrictions beyond mutual agreement. You can trade any number of resources for any other resources, make multi-party deals, or even trade for future favors. The only limit is what other players will accept.

Port trading provides better ratios than standard bank trades. Generic ports offer 3:1 trades for any resource, while specialized ports offer 2:1 trades for specific resources. Controlling ports dramatically improves your economic flexibility and trading power.

Trading Ratios

Bank Trade (No Port):

4:1 - Trade 4 identical resources for 1 of any type

Generic Port:

3:1 - Trade 3 identical resources for 1 of any type

Specialty Port (Lumber, Brick, Wheat, Ore, or Wool):

2:1 - Trade 2 specific resources for 1 of any type

Trading Strategies

  • Trade from strength: Produce abundant resources to trade for what you need
  • Control the market: Refuse trades that help the leading player
  • Don't reveal your hand: Keep building plans secret when possible
  • Negotiate early: Propose trades before rolling to secure resources in advance
  • Port advantage: Build toward ports that match your resource production

Trading Psychology

Master traders understand that every trade is information. Monitor what resources players seek and avoid, as this reveals their strategies. Use this intelligence to deny critical trades to leaders while forming advantageous alliances with trailing players.

Development Cards

Development cards introduce strategic depth and uncertainty to Catan. These cards provide powerful one-time effects, progress toward Largest Army, or award hidden victory points. The development card deck contains 25 cards in total, creating opportunities for game-changing plays.

Knight cards allow you to move the robber and steal resources, contributing toward the Largest Army achievement worth 2 victory points (requires 3 knights). Progress cards provide immediate benefits like free roads or resource monopolies. Victory Point cards remain hidden until you declare victory.

Development cards cannot be played on the turn they're purchased, adding a layer of planning and anticipation. Smart players buy development cards to maintain flexibility, secure hidden victory points, and prepare powerful moves for future turns.

Development Card Types

Purchase Cost:

  • 1 Ore

  • 1 Wheat

  • 1 Wool
Card Distribution (25 total):

  • 14 Knight cards

  • 5 Victory Point cards (1 VP each)

  • 2 Road Building cards

  • 2 Year of Plenty cards

  • 2 Monopoly cards

Card Effects Explained

1

Knight: Move the robber, block a hex, and steal a resource from an adjacent player. Three knights earn Largest Army (2 VP).

2

Victory Point: Hidden 1 VP. Keep secret until you reach 10 total points and declare victory.

3

Road Building: Place 2 free roads following normal placement rules. Excellent for claiming Longest Road or reaching new settlement locations.

4

Year of Plenty: Take any 2 resources from the bank. Perfect for completing building costs or securing scarce resources.

5

Monopoly: Name a resource; all other players give you all their cards of that type. Devastating when timed correctly.

Strategy Tips

Mastering Catan requires balancing multiple strategic layers: resource production, building efficiency, trading acumen, and tactical awareness. Advanced players plan several turns ahead while remaining flexible enough to adapt to dice rolls and opponent actions.

The early game focuses on establishing productive settlements with diverse resources. The mid-game emphasizes expansion, port control, and development card investment. The late game rewards efficient resource conversion and racing toward 10 victory points through multiple paths.

Remember that Catan is fundamentally a negotiation game. Social dynamics, alliance formation, and table politics often matter as much as mathematical optimization. The best technical play means nothing if other players unite against you through trades and robber placement.

Early Game (Turns 1-10)

  • Prioritize resource diversity in initial placement
  • Target 6 and 8 tiles for maximum production
  • Build roads aggressively to claim territory
  • Establish third settlement before upgrading cities

Mid Game (Turns 11-25)

  • Upgrade to cities on highest-producing hexes
  • Buy development cards for flexibility
  • Compete for Longest Road and Largest Army
  • Block the leader through robber placement

Late Game (Turns 25+)

  • Count visible victory points and plan accordingly
  • Form temporary alliances against the leader
  • Hide your winning turn if possible
  • Deny critical trades to players near victory

Common Mistakes

  • Over-focusing on one resource type
  • Ignoring port locations in initial placement
  • Hoarding resources instead of building
  • Making trades that primarily benefit opponents

Advanced Strategic Principles

BALANCE

Diversify production while specializing in trade

TEMPO

Build efficiently every turn to maintain momentum

ADAPTATION

Adjust strategy based on dice rolls and opponents

Winning the Game

Victory in Catan requires reaching 10 victory points before any opponent. Points come from multiple sources: settlements (1 VP), cities (2 VP), Longest Road (2 VP), Largest Army (2 VP), and certain development cards (1 VP each). Strategic players pursue multiple point sources simultaneously.

The winner is often decided by a sudden burst in the final turns. Hidden victory point cards can create surprise victories, while development cards enable powerful combination plays. Players should track visible points carefully and plan their final moves to avoid giving opponents time to counter.

Endgame awareness separates good players from great ones. Know when to transition from building infrastructure to racing for points. Understand when to form alliances against the leader versus pursuing your own victory. The last few turns often require sacrificing long-term strategy for immediate point acquisition.

Paths to Victory

The Builder (Most Common)

4 cities (8 VP) + 1 settlement (1 VP) + 1 special achievement (2 VP) = 11 VP

Focuses on upgrading cities for maximum resource production and securing one bonus achievement.

The Expander

5 settlements (5 VP) + 2 cities (4 VP) + Longest Road (2 VP) = 11 VP

Spreads across the board rapidly, controls territory, and maintains the longest road network.

The Card Player

3 cities (6 VP) + Largest Army (2 VP) + 2-3 VP cards = 10-11 VP

Invests heavily in development cards for Largest Army and hidden victory points.

The Hybrid

3 cities (6 VP) + 2 settlements (2 VP) + Both achievements (4 VP) = 12 VP

Balances multiple point sources for flexibility and resilience to opponent interference.

Final Wisdom

"Catan rewards those who build efficiently, trade shrewdly, and adapt quickly. The best strategy isn't about executing a perfect plan, it's about making the best decision with each roll of the dice."
